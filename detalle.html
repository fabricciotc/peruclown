Bouncy Bo

Bo is fascinated by the circus performers who come to her town and is inspired to become a gymnast. Her mother takes her to a gymnastics class. There, she faces many problems, from difficult friendships to her own personal struggles. Can she overcome them and gain some confidence?

The Marvelous Gymnastics Day

It is Bo's first day at gymnastics class. She is happy but also intimidated. Will she be able to balance herself on the beam?

The Wondrous Upside-Down

Bo can’t do a handstand with her grandmother instructing and criticizing her from the sidelines. She wishes that Mother would prevent Granny from visiting her gymnastics class. Why can't Granny be like Raisa's cool mom?

The Fabulous Three Musketeers

Bo becomes acquainted with a petite little tiger named Sari who joins Ms Tiana's class. The nervous little Sari makes Bo feel better, but everything changes when Sari is teamed with Bo for the gymnastics meet. Can they become friends?

The Glorious Gymnastics Queen

The big competition is approaching. A handful of gymnasts are strictly selected to compete. Bo and Raisa are picked out by Ms Tiana and Ms Eva. But who will become the glorious Gymnastics Queen?

The Miraculous Leap of Faith

Bo has to give the opening speech at the Gymnastics Fun Festival, a very special event held by the Roly-Poly Gymnastics Club. Although her mother and friends are very supportive, Bo is nervous, anxious, and shy. Can she do it?

Author notes

Clara Ng

https://twitter.com/clara_ng

This is a story about taking part in adventures and not being afraid to face the challenges that we meet. Just like the saying that “The essential thing in life is not to have conquered, but to have fought well”, these stories also highlight that the road to success is always under construction.

These stories will encourage children to see the benefits of being active, with their messages emphasizing that there is a place for everyone in sports. Sport is a vehicle to learn many life skills such as self-discipline, focus, perseverance, determination, confidence, goal-setting, delayed gratification, and risk-taking. It is also a vehicle for developing friendships and a lasting love for physical activity.

My daughter is a junior athlete in gymnastics. Although these stories are taken from many of her real-life experiences, I have added extra spices to make them relatable for everyone.
